Barnet and Southgate College is one of the largest colleges in the sector with a dynamic and aspirational corporate agenda to meet future challenges and opportunities. It has recently reviewed its Governance model following its strategic merger in November 2011 and has decided to move to a Policy Governance model from September 2013. This model will result in a smaller, more focussed Board, committed to making the College one of the very best, dedicated to improving the learner experience and services to business.

As a result the College is now looking for Board Members who are able to offer high-level strategic skills including executive monitoring and scrutiny, strategic planning and vision, leadership and direction on the drive to raise standards and an ability to act effectively as “critical friends” to the Principal and Executive Leadership Group. Applications for these new roles are invited from individuals who have advanced scrutiny and strategic decision making skills, together with local or national contacts and knowledge and a personal commitment to strategic leadership. The College is particularly interested to hear from people who have a background in one or more of the following areas:

  • General Education Management / Higher Education
  • Property Development /Capital Projects
  • Financial/Risk Management and/or mitigation
  • Charity Regulation and Practice
  • Management Consultancy

The College represents a diverse community and is therefore looking to reflect this diversity within the new Board, whilst achieving a broad and balanced range of expertise and experience.

If you are interested in this new and exciting opportunity, further information about the Board Member role and the College can be read in the recruitment pack. The application form can be downloaded here. Also attached for your information is the latest Annual Report.

Saltdean Lido Saltdean Lido on the south Sussex coast is a beautiful example of an art deco lido, buildings that became a feature of many British seaside resorts during in the 1930s when outdoor swimming was a highly fashionable sport. Local councils built them to cater for higher numbers of holidaymakers and in a response to strict bathing restrictions and charges that applied to public beaches and offered bathing pools, stylish diving platforms and waterchutes, sun terraces for lounging and relaxing and on-site cafes and restaurants. Their popularity remained until the mid-1930s and then as modern tastes changed and attendances dropped, many sadly fell into disrepair were eventually demolished. Click to read more.

Saltdean on the other hand is currently being supported by a campaign started by local residents after plans were announced to fill in the swimming pool and develop the site into residential flats. A CIC has been set up to safeguard the site and have it provide a range of leisure and recreational activities throughout the year rather than just seasonally and to establish the Lido as the community hub it was always intended to be and a must-see visitor attraction. Brighton & Hove City Council are offering a 50 year lease which the CIC is currently bidding to win in partnership with the Wave Leisure Trust, a non-profit leisure operator. The CIC has being enjoying successes so far: it has already successfully passed the first stage of bidding and earlier this year was awarded National Lottery funding to help with the annual Saltdean Lido Fun Day, supported by c10,000 local residents. This year’s celebrations also marked the lido’s 75th birthday. More info about the bidding process can be found at Saltdean Lido CIC.

The current board are hoping to strengthen their skillset through the appointment of another volunteer non-exec director with an experienced finance background, to play a full part in the strategic direction of the CIC. The successful candidate will lead on the financial aspects related to the future regeneration and operation of the Lido. As well as the appropriate financial background and qualifications, the ideal candidate would also have experience in start-up ventures and investment, or fundraising in the public and/or private sector and be able to establish confidence in the company’s accountancy & budgetary procedures. This role offers a unique opportunity to contribute to preserving local heritage for future generations.

More information about the role remit can be found here.

Bladder and Bowel Foundation (BBF) Bladder and Bowel Foundation (BBF) is the leading support and advocacy charity for people with bladder and bowel control problems, their families, carers and healthcare professionals.  There is an estimated 14 million people in the UK with a bladder control problem, and 6.5 million with a bowel control problem.  Bladder and Bowel Foundation works to make a positive difference in the lives of people affected by such problems.  The Charity raises awareness, educates and empowers the general public and healthcare professionals, promotes and assists research, and seeks to ensure that people with bladder and bowel problems have access to treatments and services they need. Click to read more.

The charity is currently seeking a new Chair who play a critical role in leading an ambitious growth strategy to ensure the issues of bladder and bowel control are fully recognised and resourced to match the scale of problems.  The Chair will work closely with the Chief Executive in managing key relationships and to ensure the Board’s cohesiveness, effectiveness and contribution in achieving its objectives. This person must be someone who brings excellent strategic skills to shape the on-going work of the organisation.
 
They are also seeking a number of Trustees who will work with the incoming Chair and help him or her set the strategic direction of the Foundation and ensuring that their ambition to make a real difference to the lives of people with bladder and bowel problems is realised. The Trustees will need to contribute constructively to discussion of the organisations work and practice good governance principles in being assured that the Foundations objects are being met.  At a time of significant change, they’re looking for Trustees who will lead and encourage the executive team and enhance the understanding of key stakeholder groups.  BBF is particularly interested in candidates with personal or professional clinical experience of bladder and bowel issues and people with sound charity and/or business governance and financial management experience.  
 
Time commitments: The Board meets four times each year although it is expected that Trustees will also participate in sub-committees,  contribute time in promoting the objects of the Charity and keeping in touch with the needs of those affected by bladder and bowel control problems.
