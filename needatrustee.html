Want a Non-Executive

Non-Executives Unlimited offers different levels of service depending on your requirements and your size. We source potential candidates from multiple channels including from our own database of over 2,000 senior people who come from the private, public and civil society sectors.

We also search through other channels including through our strategic partners such as Centrica, the Financial Times Non Executive Club, Accenture, KPMG, British Telecom, the National Union of Students, Black on Board, Getting on Board, Women Like Us, the Institute of Fundraising and Russam GMS's database of 12,000 senior executives. Click here to view a breakdown of our database. We also post the vacancy on various websites including NCVO’s Trustee Bank, Charity Job and Timewise and use social media, and in particular LinkedIn and Twitter, to promote the roles.

Non-Executives Unlimited offers a simple and adaptable process and for smaller organisations (organisations with a turnover less than £1m) a Basic Service. For larger organisations we offer a Standard Service or a full Search & Selection. Of course, you may just wish to Advertise on the site.

Taking the brief is the most important element of any recruitment process and we will want to make sure we really understand the requirements of the role(s). For the Standard Service this will involve at least one face to face meeting with the Chair/Chief Executive or senior trustee in charge of the selection process and a conversation with all the members of the nominations committee. We are happy to help create a recruitment pack to go with the campaign which will include a person specification and role description and we commit to keeping you informed of progress throughout the process.

Candidates are asked to address how they meet the requirements of the person specification in their application. For the Standard and Search & Selection process Non-Executives Unlimited will conduct an initial sift of the applicants and we will interview long listed candidates. Sometimes organisations wish to review all applications and instruct us who to interview and other times organisations leave the initial long listing to our judgement.

Reports are written on the interviewed candidates and you will be presented with a report against which we can help you can short list. We aim to deliver a recommended shortlist within 6 weeks of taking the brief although we can be flexible on the timeline. We will take references for short listed candidates and are happy to help along all steps in the process.

The Basic Service is for organisations with a turnover of less than £1m. Following a one hour briefing conversation (usually on the phone) between the selection committee or nominated person of the recruiting organisation and Non-Executives Unlimited, a search will be conducted of the Non-Executives Unlimited database and other channels. Candidates who express an interest in the role will have their unvetted details forwarded on to the recruiting organisation.

The Standard Service consists of the recruitment process as outlined above. The appointed Non-Executive(s) will have a free place at a half-day induction workshop run jointly by BWB/NCVO which occurs twice a year and a free place as a delegate at the next NCVO/BWB Governance Conference.

The Search & Selection service is akin to a traditional head hunt although it has the advantage of building on all the various routes to market we have already established. It means that no stone is left unturned in your pursuit of the right candidate. For a senior Chair role or for a specific skill set that is hard to find, this could be the way to go.

NCVO members – any NCVO member organisation using the basic or standard service will also have their vacancies posted in the NCVO members e-bulletin.

Non-Executives Unlimited has been set up in such a way so that a wide variety of organisations can use its services cost-effectively. We are aware of the financial commitment required to undertake a recruitment process of this nature and so we have tried to remove the financial risk by ensuring that, apart from a small initial engagement fee, the balance is only payable on successful appointment.
