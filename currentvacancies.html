The Cyclopark Trust: New Chair and New Board Trustees

The Cyclopark Trust was created to operate Cyclopark, one of the largest cycle sports parks in Europe.  It is part of Kent’s Olympic legacy and since opening has attracted more than 250,000 visitors, and hosted some of the UK’s biggest cycling events at what has become one of the premier race track facilities in the UK and Europe.
Click to read more.

Our principle objectives are the advancement of education, health and sport through the provision of high quality, accessible and affordable facilities, and in developing programmes and events for the communities we serve. The charity has some ambitious aims: for example, we have developed Cyclokids, one of the most popular play parks in the local area but would like to develop this area still further by installing additional equipment so that the sensory garden zone offers more interest to those with sensory disabilities. The park already offers one of the most inclusive facilities for cycle sports in the country with its fleet of adaptive bikes, changing hoists, and fully trained staff, and would like to be able to purchase additional bikes to meet the needs of both profoundly disabled as well as those with less complex needs. In partnership with our host club, Club Cyclopark we want to develop individual talent so that we can compete at local national or indeed on the international stage, through the development of a youth cycling team.
 
Currently, the Trust has exceeded expectations in its growth forecast and now has an annual turnover of almost £700,000 employing 13 FTE staff, providing an on-site café, retail and health treatment facilities. Like any new business start up the Trust’s approach has been very entrepreneurial and it has worked hard in securing business partnerships with a number of local and national businesses to develop Cyclopark as a multi-sport destination. The Cyclopark Trust now seeks to recruit a new Chair of the board and new board trustees to lead the Trust into the next phase of its development and business evolution. The Chair will bring a senior level of strategic experience and be skilled in brokering and developing lasting partnerships, and the trustees will ideally have experience in marketing and communications, or business development, with experience in fundraising for sports at a community level a distinct advantage.  Applicants who have experience working in local or regional politics are also encouraged to apply, as are those who hold a qualification in accountancy with sound financial management experience.
 
Full information about each role can be downloaded here: Cyclopark candidate pack and please follow the relevant application link for either the Chair or trustee role.
 
The closing date for applications is Monday 26th January 2015 and applicants are kindly reminded to note interview and site visit dates listed in the Trustee Recruitment Pack
 
Note that this is, like most trustee positions, an unremunerated role. Trustees Unlimited will, of course, respect the privacy of any conversations regarding this role.

 


Policy and Campaigning expertise sought for leading international aid charity ActionAid

ActionAid UK is looking for a trustee with an interest in policy, advocacy and campaigning. This role represents a fantastic opportunity for an experienced professional who wants to be part of an organisation that is delivering a bold strategy to provide sustained improvements to the poorest and most excluded people in the world.
Click to read more.

As a member of our Board of Trustees, you will have enormous influence over the success of our organisation. With your fellow trustees, you will shape our long-term strategy and be guardians of our ethos and values, stewarding income from supporters and institutions, influencing change and assisting other parts of our global federation.

You will ensure that we are led and managed effectively, take the well-being of our employees and volunteers seriously and are accountable to our stakeholders. All of this is essential if we are to play an influential and exemplary role as a member of ActionAid’s international federation and maintain our justifiably high level of public regard and respect across the globe.

As well as embracing our aims and values, you must have a good understanding of charity governance issues and the ability to demonstrate understanding of the trustee role from a non-executive perspective. We will need in-depth knowledge of human rights based approaches including familiarity with women’s rights and gender equality issues. Vital also will be experience of engaging as a campaigner and/or as a policy advocate on issues relevant to Action Aid’s mission and strategic objectives. On a personal level you will bring strategic leadership expertise, networking capabilities and relationship building skills.

Time Commitment

The likely time commitment for the role will be 20 days per year which will include four all-day Board meetings, a Board retreat with an overnight stay plus relevant committee meetings. Ideally, you will also make at least one visit to an ActionAid country programme. This role will take time and dedication, but you will be influencing the steps we take to help build a better world.

Additional Members of Allergy UK Board of Trustees Sought

Allergy UK is the leading national charity dedicated to supporting the estimated 21 million allergy sufferers in the UK, as well as those with food intolerances.  Find out more about us on our website www.allergyuk.org
Click to read more.

Applications are invited for additional members of Allergy UK Board of Trustees.  We are looking for people who are able to work effectively as part of a team, with strong communication and interpersonal skills.  It is also important that candidates can demonstrate a track record not only of formulating plans, but also of transforming those plans into actions.

Applications from individuals with one or more of the following skills and experience will be particularly welcome

  • Strong finance skills
  • Good understanding of legal issues affecting charities
  • Fund-raising and income-generation
  • Lobbying

An understanding of allergy, although not strictly necessary, is a distinct advantage, whether that understanding is through clinical training or through direct or indirect personal experience of allergy or intolerance.

Like many trustee positions, our board appointments are unremunerated, although of course expenses will be reimbursed.
 
Please contact Jane Josh for an application pack containing further information about the trustee opportunities and the application process: jane@allergyuk.org
 
Please remember to put the following reference in the subject line of your email: TU-AllergyUK.

Closing date: Monday 15th December 2014.
