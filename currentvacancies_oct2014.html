The Phyllis Tuckwell Hospice (PTH) - Income Generation Trustee

The Phyllis Tuckwell Hospice (PTH) offers specialist palliative care in a compassionate and friendly environment. As an independent charitable trust and a centre of excellence, we aim to provide equitable supportive care to those with life-limiting illness and to their families. Our services help more than 1000 people each year and include an 18 bed in-patient unit, a day care centre, a team of Community Nurse Specialists and a Hospice Care at Home service.
Click to read more.

Based in Farnham, Surrey, we serve an area covering South West Surrey and North East Hampshire.  In order to maintain and expand our services we need to raise £7m a year and rely heavily on the local communities that we serve to support us. Over 85% of our annual income comes from charitable donations including our 15 charity shops and 2 furniture showrooms, a whole range of fundraising activities and income from the Tuckwell CHASE Lottery (a joint venture with Shooting Star CHASE Children’s Hospice). The challenge facing Trustees and management is to continue to sustain and develop our services whilst strengthening and expanding sustainable sources of income.

Dynamic Chair sought for pioneering medical charity

Ehlers-Danlos Support UK (EDS UK) was established in 1987 to support, advise and inform those living with Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome (EDS) and to help them live a full, active and positive life.   EDS UK is the only UK based charity that exclusively represents and supports all people with any of the types of EDS regardless of their position on the EDS spectrum.EDS is a genetic connective tissue condition known as  a multi-systemic syndrome.  Connective tissue covers over 90% of one’s body so there are few bodily systems which escape the effects.  Within the 6 main types of EDS there is a spectrum of symptoms with one of the types being life-limiting.
Click to read more.

EDS UK runs regular events and conferences to bring their community together and produces a variety of literature to increase understanding in managing the condition and merchandise to sell to help fund the charity’s work. EDS UK strives to educate the medical community, promoting “joined up” medical care, treatment, management and improve accurate diagnosis. We fund vital research to further understand EDS, finding the gene responsible and working towards a cure, and we provide literature for patients, carers, teachers, social services and the medical profession.
 
We are a lifeline to those isolated by the condition and provide non-medical advice on the condition.  We run residential and one day conferences where patients/carers meet socially, feel comfortable with those who understand, and hear numerous medical professionals speak on the many aspects of EDS.  We also run a help-line from the office and have 45 Area-Coordinators nationally who organise meetings and are a local point of contact for members.  More information about how EDS UK can help advise EDS sufferers and their families can be found here: EDS UK - Useful Information
 
The last few years has seen an exciting period of growth and development for EDS UK.  We employ four members of staff and 2 consultants and have a large volunteer network, including 45 Area Coordinator volunteers and a number of office volunteers.  There are approximately 1,800 EDS UK members and growing.  A dynamic Chair is now needed to take the charity to the next stage of the charity’s development. This person need not experience EDS themselves, but must be willing to undertake the necessary learning to become acquainted with the basics of EDS so that they can represent the organisation effectively to external audiences. They must be able to demonstrate the right level of strategic and business skills necessary to help drive the charity forward and bring gravitas to hold credibility with the EDS UK membership, supporters and the Medical Advisory Panel, so that these groups have confidence that the aims and vision of the organisation are being communicated and upheld in order to sustain support for the future.

The Association of MBAs (AMBA)

West Essex Mind (WEM) is a local charity working across the West Essex district to support people affected by mental ill health, and the people around them, on the journey towards recovery. Affiliated to National Mind, WEM offers services that are sensitive to local needs: we believe that with the appropriate support everyone has the potential to take steps that will improve their quality of life and are committed to finding ways of developing these activities in a way that makes them responsive to the needs of the local population.
Click to read more.

We aim to achieve this by providing a range of different community-based activities and opportunities. This includes access to Talking Therapies, whereby we help people look at their own skills and resources and feel more empowered to use them to help make changes and resolve difficulties, a Youth Support Service for young people who might be feeling the pressures of growing up, exams, peer pressure, etc., and the Building Bridges programme for local people with a mental health issue feel more involved in their local community.
 
Our Board of Trustees is drawn from the local community and are currently seeking similar new board members to help develop WEM services for the future. Applicants need not necessarily have direct experience of mental ill health but must be able to demonstrate empathy for helping to overcome the stigma surrounding it and who understand the importance of maintaining access to services at a local level. People who can bring skills and/or access to networks in this field or from experience in working with other vulnerable groups are encouraged to apply, as well as people with experience in generating alternative income streams; local authority funding is due to end later this year and so it is critical the WEM have other sustainable income in place for the future. The Association of MBAs (AMBA)

The Association of MBAs (AMBA) is the globally-renowned authority on postgraduate business education. It has an international reputation for excellence and impartiality, underpinned by its accreditation service, which is recognised as the global standard for all MBA, DBA and MBM programmes.   AMBA is the only membership organisation for accredited MBAs, connecting employers, business schools, students and alumni worldwide. Over the past 20 years AMBA has grown considerably, expanding from programmes accredited in 31 business schools (of which 10% were International) to 210 business schools (includes Potchefstroom Business School, South Africa, USP Fiji and Nankai University, China) in over 70 countries across the globe. This represents a shift from what was a primarily UK-focused organisation to a truly international organisation. AMBA unveiled an ambitious and exciting new Strategy in 2014 and is now embarking on its delivery to realise the growth of the organisation all areas, which includes not only increasing the number of  accredited programmes across the world but also substantively improving the membership package with a view to attract and increase a global membership mix. AMBA has recently strengthened its non-executive representation from the business community by appointing Corporate Trustees with the vision and experience to contribute to these key issues.
Click to read more.

The current Chair is retiring in September and the current Hon Treasurer is stepping up into this role, so the Association is now recruiting a new Hon Treasurer, an individual who is passionate about the delivery of quality business education to help guide AMBA at a strategic level. This person will bring expert financial analysis skills and hold a relevant financial qualification, as well as an MBA.  They will also possess first-rate communication skills and experience of other membership and/or educational bodies would be an advantage. The role will involve monitoring the financial administration of AMBA and report succinctly on its state of financial health, as well as holding responsibility for overseeing and presenting budgets, internal management accounts and annual financial statements and adhering to all governance, legal and regulatory requirements.

Prospect Housing and Support Services

Prospect Housing and Support Services was formed in 1989 by several local organisations and authorities, who wanted to improve housing facilities for people with learning disabilities and mental health problems, so they could live independently. The organisation now runs a variety independent living schemes for vulnerable adults and people with physical and learning disabilities and other special needs. Click to read more.

Their current properties and projects in Mid and East Surrey and the London Borough of Sutton include: nine residential care homes for people with learning disabilities, a care home for carers of young people (18 – 30 yrs) with learning disabilities and supported independent living for people with a learning disability, acquired brain injury, mental ill-health, sensory impairment, and people with Asperger’s Syndrome, as well as other residential and domiciliary support to over 40 young people with a learning disability to give their carers a break.

The charity’s mission is to enable people who use its services to realise their potential and to exercise choice and control over their own lives and their values include allowing people to make informed choices about where they live and who to live with, their work, money, support and involvement in the local community and they work to safeguard the legal and civil rights, including the rights to vote, marry, have a family, express opinions and be protected from abuse, of everyone who uses their services.

The charity is just about to appoint several new trustees to the board but still has one other vacancy to fill. In this instance they're are looking specifically for someone who has worked at a senior level in social services, the NHS, in commissioning or in specialist education.
