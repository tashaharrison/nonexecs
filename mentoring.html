Mentoring

Non-Executives Unlimited offers a mentoring service for individuals on boards. Many individuals, especially those new to board membership or Chairs, find mentoring an extremely helpful tool. A mentor is a very experienced individual who will meet with the mentee at regular intervals over an agreed period to discuss with them the challenges and issues they face.

We help broker the relationship between mentor and mentee and provide quality assurance to ensure that the relationship is adding real value. The numbers of sessions are dictated by the mentee and there is no minimum number.

We have a small pool of trained and experienced mentors and their details are below.

Stephen Brooker

Stephen Brooker is Chairman of the Russam GMS Not for Profit and Health Practices. Stephen has been working in the Charity and Not for Profit sectors since 1990 when he was recruited to the British Red Cross Society as Director of Finance, having previously been a partner in Ernst & Young. From the Red Cross, Stephen went to Emmanuel College, Cambridge as Bursar and Fellow. In his current work he has a wide range of clients including ACEVO, for whom he holds a monthly finance surgery at which Chief Executives seek advice on finance, fundraising and longer-term strategic issues. He is a non-executive director of East & North Herts NHS Trust, chairing their Audit Committee. He is also external Chair of the Audit Committee of the Law Society and Stephen was an external member of the Audit Committee of the University of Cambridge for 13 years. He is a Trustee of several national charities and a founding Trustee of the Honorary Treasurers' Forum. Stephen has just been appointed to the Audit Committee of the House of Commons.

Mike Whitlam

Mike Whitlam CBE has worked at the top of the Voluntary Sector for over 30 years, his original management training being from Manchester Business School and his M.Phil from Cranfield. He was CEO of the British Red Cross and of the RNID, Mentor Foundation (International) and Vision 2020 the Right to Sight Campaign. Other organisations where he has held a senior post include NACRO, Save the Children and Prisoners Abroad, where he was Chair. He was the Founder of ACEVO and works with a wide range of charity sector clients on strategy, management and people issues. His unrivalled personal reputation and his broad range of influential contacts add immeasurably to the effectiveness and value of work that Russam GMS do in the Charity Sector. He is also currently a Non-Executive Director of an NHS Primary Care Trust and Chair of the Ofcom Advisory Committee for disabled people. He has taken part in many TV and Radio programmes as well as lectured and written widely on the voluntary sector.

Mike is involved with several International and National Charities including Watford FC Community Trust, where he is also a Season Ticket holder. When he has spare time he sculpts in Wood and also paints.
